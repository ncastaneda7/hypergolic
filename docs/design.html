The Worse Engine

Why?

I want to learn how to design rocket engines, and the best way to achieve this is by just doing it. I'm not a propulsion engineer, yet.

Worse = Best

1) My goal is to understand the basic principles and applications in the context of a propulsion system, and designing components in isolation will not provide this knowledge, therefore arriving to a complete design > best design. To achieve this I'll need to make many assumptions and simplifications to get to a design quicker, regardless of how "good" or "bad" it'll be (with some common sense obviously - I don't want things melting or breaking).

2) Since I've never done this before I'm giving myself permission to fail. It's performance might the "worse", but I'll strive for the engineering and science behind the design will be as realistic as possible, and the "best" I can do as my first time doing this. I think about this like a sandbox to play and explore.
